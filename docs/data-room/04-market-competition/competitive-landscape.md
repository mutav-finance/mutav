# Competitive Landscape

---

## Summary

No direct competitor combines both dimensions MUTAV occupies: Brazil's rental guarantee market and the global onchain RWA category. The closest analogues are structurally different enough that MUTAV holds a genuinely open position.

**The 10-word version:** *Maple Finance for Brazilian rent defaults.*

---

## Brazilian Rental Guarantee Market

### Traditional Insurance (Seguro Fiança)

| Player | Key Data |
|---|---|
| Tokio Marine | +52.1% growth in fiança (2024); total profit R$1.4B ($280M) |
| Porto Seguro | Top 3 across real estate verticals |
| Pottencial | R$1.9B (~$380M) in fiança segment over 12 months |

These operate under SUSEP as underwriters — entirely internal capital, no investment instruments, no tokenization. MUTAV is not replacing insurance; it operates under a different legal basis (fiança, not seguro) and does not compete for the same regulatory bucket.

### Digital Garantidoras / Fintechs

| Player | Status | Key Data |
|---|---|---|
| **CredAluga** | Fast-growing | +200% in 2024; 30,000 contracts; R$30M AUM ($6M); raised R$60M (~$12M) (Sep 2024); Grupo OLX shareholder |
| **QuintoAndar** | Exited segment (2024) | Left a gap that competitors are scrambling to fill |
| **CredAluga + Kanastra** | Fund product | First investment fund replacing título de capitalização — closest structural analogue to MUTAV |

**CredAluga is the most relevant benchmark:**

| Dimension | CredAluga | MUTAV |
|---|---|---|
| Capital structure | Closed (VC-backed) | Open (tokenized, external investors) |
| Transparency | Opaque | Fully onchain (NAV verifiable by anyone) |
| Investor access | Accredited institutional only | Any KYC-cleared investor globally |
| Default settlement | Manual bank processes | Programmatic (smart contract, 5-day SLA) |
| Tranche architecture | None identified | MTVH / MTVM / MTVL (waterfall) |
| Distribution moat | OLX Grupo (scale) | Cinara network (Sul); relationship-driven |

CredAluga is well-capitalized with a head start. MUTAV's structural advantage: we can attract external capital they cannot access because their model is not designed for it.

## Global RWA / Onchain Credit Protocols

| Protocol | Asset Class | AUM | vs. MUTAV |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Maple Finance** | Institutional uncollateralized credit | $600M+ | Same structure (pool + tranches), different underlying |
| **Centrifuge** | Structured credit, trade receivables | $450M+ | Institutional-only; much more complex |
| **Goldfinch** | Emerging market SME credit (LatAm, Africa) | $100M+ | Geographic overlap; different asset class |
| **Ondo Finance** | Tokenized US Treasuries | $700M+ | Yield only; no real-world credit exposure |

MUTAV vs. these protocols: more specific cash flow (rental fees are contractual and recurring), more defensible distribution (74,000 imobiliárias in Brazil cannot be accessed remotely), lower market correlation (default rates don't track crypto cycles), and unclaimed territory (none have Brazilian rental).
