# Market Opportunity

> Sources: SUSEP, IBGE/PNAD Contínua 2024, FipeZap, Superlógica, ABMI, Universal Software, CNseg, FenaCap. Compiled May 2026.

---

## Summary

Brazil has 17.8M rented households. The rental guarantee market grew 195% since 2020 and now generates ~$1.1B/year (~R$5.4B) in fees across all paid modalities. No dominant winner after QuintoAndar's 2024 exit from the segment. Capital entirely closed to external investors.

MUTAV's TAM is not the rental market — it is the capital infrastructure layer behind it: ~R$6-9B ($1.2B-$1.8B) annually sitting on insurance balance sheets and private fintech funds, inaccessible to anyone outside that circle.

## The Guarantee Market

### Shift from Personal Guarantor to Paid Guarantees

| Year | Personal guarantor (fiador) | Paid guarantees | Other |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2020 | 62.0% | 18.1% | 19.9% |
| 2024 | 39.5% | **48.9%** | 11.6% |

Paid guarantees surpassed the personal guarantor as the dominant modality in 2024. The shift is structural and accelerating.

### Market Volume

| Type | Annual volume | USD equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Surety insurance (Seguro Fiança) | R$1.9B/year | ~$380M |
| Capitalização guarantee title | ~R$3.2B/year | ~$640M |
| Digital fintechs (CredAluga, etc.) | ~R$300M/year | ~$60M |
| **Total paid guarantees** | **~R$5.4B/year** | **~$1.08B** |

Growth: Surety insurance +195% (2020–2024); total paid guarantees +254% (2020–2024). Record month: R$174.5M ($34.9M) in February 2025.

Default rates rising (3.14% Sep 2024 → 3.80% Sep 2025) — a tailwind: higher defaults increase demand for guarantees. Sul region is the lowest in Brazil at **2.41%**, ideal for the pilot.

## Why Now

**1. Market gap:** QuintoAndar exited the guarantee segment in 2024. CredPago absorbed by Loft. The next cycle's winner is undecided.

**2. The CredPago blueprint:** Acquired by Loft (July 2021) with 123,000 contracts, R$40B ($8B) in assets under guarantee, 16,000 imobiliária partners. BTG Pactual's investment returned **R$1.4 billion (~$280M) in under 2 years**. That was a closed capital model. MUTAV is what CredPago could have been if the capital structure were open.

**3. Despejo Extrajudicial Law (PL 3.999/20):** CCJ approved June 2025, awaiting Senate. Accelerates eviction timelines — making guarantees more powerful and increasing demand for the capital behind them.

**4. High Selic → more renters:** +44.7% growth in rented households since 2016 with no reversal in sight.

**5. Blockchain credit model proven globally:** Maple, Centrifuge, and Goldfinch validated that credit instruments can be managed on blockchain. None touched Brazilian rental. MUTAV applies the model to a market that has never seen it.
