# ETHGlobal Hackathon FAQ

Refer to this file when users ask about hackathon rules, eligibility, staking, teams, applications, or general event questions.

---

## General

**Who can attend?**
ETHGlobal events are for developers and builders who want to create or contribute to projects during the hackathon.

**How much does it cost?**
Online hackathons are free to attend. Hackers must stake 0.005 ETH to confirm their spot. The stake is fully returned as long as you participate and submit a project (even partial) by the close of the event.

**I'm new to Ethereum — can I still participate?**
Yes! Events are open to both beginners and veterans. Events are tailored to attendees with Web2 experience who want to build on Web3 for the first time.

**What is this hackathon?**
It brings together top minds in the Ethereum ecosystem to tackle challenges defining the next evolution of Web3. Attendees build alongside mentors and partners and present their work to respected industry leaders.

**Where can I find all projects ever built at an ETHGlobal event?**
At the ETHGlobal Showcase page (https://ethglobal.com/showcase).

**Where can I find video content?**
All summits, workshops, and recorded content are available here: https://www.youtube.com/c/ETHGlobal/playlists.

**How can I partner or collaborate with ETHGlobal?**
Email the Partnerships team at partner@ethglobal.com.

## Event Rules

**Can I participate remotely?**
Depends on the event — virtual events are fully remote. IRL events require in-person attendance.

**Can I start working on my project before the event?**
No. Projects must be started from scratch when the hackathon officially begins. You can familiarize yourself with tools and technologies beforehand, but no coding or design work on your project.

**Can I use AI to build my project?**
Yes, with guidelines:
- **Attribution**: Clearly document where and how AI tools were used (which files, code sections, or assets).
- **Involvement**: AI should assist your process, not create the entire project. Submissions relying entirely on AI without meaningful team contributions may not be eligible for partner prizes or finalist consideration.

**How many Partner Prizes can I apply for?**
Maximum 3 Partner Prizes per project. If a partner has multiple tracks, all tracks count as just 1 Partner Prize. The ETHGlobal Finalist Track is separate and does not count toward the 3-prize limit.

**When must I stop working on my project?**
Once submission closes, stop working until prizes are officially distributed.

**If I work on my project before the hackathon, am I eligible for partner prizes?**
ETHGlobal does not select partner prizes — partners manage their own selection. Partners can award prizes to any project that meets their bounty requirements, regardless of when work began. However, working before the event disqualifies you from being an ETHGlobal Finalist.

**Who do I go to for help?**
- Mentors page on your hacker dashboard
- `#mentorship-help` Discord channel
- Partner-specific Discord channels for sponsor questions
- General questions: hello@ethglobal.com

**Can I edit my project after creating it?**
Yes, you can edit name, description, etc. as many times as you like up until the submission deadline.

**What is Project Check-in?**
An opportunity for your team to get insight and feedback on your project during the hackathon and get unblocked on any issues.

**How can I remove someone from my team?**
Email hello@ethglobal.com and they'll take care of it.

## Hacker Application

**How many members can be on a team?**
Maximum 5 members. Everyone must apply and stake separately as an individual. Solo participation is allowed but teaming up (at least 2 people) is recommended.

**Do I need to apply even if I'm already part of a team?**
Yes. Every individual must apply and stake separately. Each application is reviewed individually.

**Can I hack solo?**
Yes, though teaming up is encouraged.

**I don't have a team yet — should I be worried?**
No, but form a team before the hackathon starts to make the best use of your time. Use the Team Formation & Brainstorming sessions, the `#find-a-team` Discord channel, or your Hacker Dashboard to connect with others.

**I don't have any ideas — help!**
Attend the Team Formation & Brainstorming sessions, check partner pages for inspiration, browse Discord channels, or review the Resources Guide (https://ethglobal.com/events/hackmoney2026/info/resources).

**Tips for a successful application?**
- Provide as much detail as possible, especially around your coding ability and GitHub profile
- Keep your CV up to date and clearly articulate what you want to build
- Apply early — applications are reviewed in batches
- Online hackathons have more spots than IRL events (not capacity-constrained)

**Why haven't I heard back about my application?**
Applications are reviewed in batches. All applications will be reviewed.

**Can I change my wallet address?**
Yes — go to the Wallet Confirmation section on your hacker dashboard and follow the steps.

**Can I change my email address?**
Email hello@ethglobal.com from your existing address with the new one you want. Must be a fresh email — accounts cannot be merged.

**My friend/teammate missed the deadline for a virtual hackathon — can they still join?**
If they've been accepted, they can still stake their spot and join your team.

## Staking

**My dashboard still says "Stake" after I staked — what do I do?**
Email hello@ethglobal.com with a link to your successful transaction.

**I accidentally double-staked — what do I do?**
Email hello@ethglobal.com with both transaction hashes and they'll sort it out.

**Why is my stake stuck pending?**
This happens when you navigate away from the dashboard before the transaction confirms. ETHGlobal checks for stakes submitted in the last 24 hours — if you don't return to confirm within that window, it won't register. Email hello@ethglobal.com.

**When will I get my stake back?**
Stakes are returned in batches after the event ends, generally within three weeks.

**I can no longer attend — can I get my stake back?**
Yes. Email hello@ethglobal.com. Your application will be withdrawn and your stake marked for return (within ~two weeks after the event ends).

**I don't see my returned stake transaction — where is it?**
Check the Internal Transactions tab on Etherscan, or look under other scan sites (BscScan, PolygonScan, Optimistic Ethereum, GnosisScan, etc.) depending on which network your stake was on.

## Mentoring & Volunteering

**What is the role of a mentor?**
Mentors are highly technical resources for hackers. They attend team formation and brainstorming sessions, project feedback sessions, and respond to questions on Discord. They provide guidance and advice to hackers as they build.
